# EA Eventos — Content Audit & Initial Content Strategy

## Project Overview

EA Eventos is a local event audio services company specializing in sound systems, lighting, DJ services and event production.

As part of the company's digital marketing initiative, a collection of event videos was received after a wedding event. The objective was to evaluate the available material and determine whether it could be used to support future social media campaigns.

---

# Business Problem

The company needed content for its social media channels but lacked:

- A formal content strategy.
- A content calendar.
- A visual identity guide.
- Professional event photography.
- A structured content generation process.

At the time of the analysis, the only available marketing assets were video clips recorded during a wedding event.

---

# Initial Situation

## Available Assets

- 43 video clips from a wedding event.
- No usable photography.
- No promotional footage.
- No backstage footage.
- No equipment showcase footage.
- No customer testimonials.

### Additional Challenges

The videos were recorded by an inexperienced content creator during their first event.

Common issues included:

- Inconsistent framing.
- Excessive camera movement.
- Direct exposure to stage lighting.
- Short clip duration.
- Limited crowd visibility.

---

# Objective

Evaluate all available video clips and identify the maximum amount of usable content that could support future marketing efforts.

---

# Methodology

A content audit process was designed to classify each video according to its potential marketing value.

## Classification System

### Category A — Ready to Use

Videos with:

- Acceptable framing.
- Visible audience engagement.
- Functional lighting effects.
- Sufficient duration for editing.

### Category B — Recoverable

Videos requiring editing or trimming but still containing useful moments.

### Category C — Archive

Videos discarded from marketing use due to quality limitations.

---

# Audit Results

| Category | Description | Quantity |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| A | Ready to Use | 6 |
| B | Recoverable | 20 |
| C | Archive | 17 |

Total videos analyzed: **43**

---

# Main Findings

Most of the available material could not be used directly for publication.

The most common reasons for rejection were:

### Short Duration

Several clips ended before meaningful action occurred.

### Limited Audience Presence

Some videos showed only a small portion of the dance floor, reducing perceived event energy.

### Lighting Issues

Multiple recordings pointed directly at event lighting fixtures, causing visual discomfort and reducing content quality.

### Camera Positioning

Inconsistent framing reduced the ability to showcase the atmosphere of the event.

---

# Key Insight

The analysis revealed that the primary challenge was not content quantity, but content acquisition quality.

Although more than 40 videos were available, only a small percentage met minimum standards for direct marketing use.

This highlighted the need for a structured content capture process for future events.

---

# Recommendations

## Short Term

- Organize Category A and B videos into a content library.
- Prioritize editing the six Category A clips.
- Create social media content using the strongest event moments.

## Long Term

Develop a content generation framework including:

- Event recording guidelines.
- Shot lists.
- Content capture checklists.
- Storage and organization standards.

This process aims to improve content quality and reduce future marketing bottlenecks.
